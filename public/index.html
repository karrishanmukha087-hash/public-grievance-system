About the Portal

The Public Grievance System is a digital governance platform designed to make state departments accountable and accessible. Our system acts as a bridge between the citizens and government bodies like Municipal Corporations, Electricity Boards, and Public Health departments.

Our Objective

  • Provide a single, unified gateway for lodging public grievances.
  • Ensure fast-tracked, time-bound resolution monitored via strict SLAs.
  • Provide citizens with complete real-time tracking transparency.
  • Equip administrators with powerful analytics to monitor department performances.

Frequently Asked Questions

Get quick answers to common queries regarding lodging and tracking grievances

What is the standard time-frame for resolving a complaint? +

Resolutions are bound by specific Service Level Agreements (SLAs) depending on the category. For example, Sanitation complaints are addressed in 3 days, Electricity issues in 5 days, Water supply in 7 days, and Roads in 15 days.

Can I upload photographs of the local issues? +

Yes. The portal allows citizens to upload supporting documents or photos in PNG, JPG, JPEG, or PDF format. The maximum file size permitted is 2MB.

What happens after I lodge a grievance? +

Upon submission, a unique Complaint ID is auto-generated (e.g., COMP-2026-000001). The grievance is instantly routed to the concerned department, and you will receive notifications in your dashboard as soon as the status changes.

Can I edit or delete my grievance after lodging it? +

You can edit or delete your grievance details anytime from your panel, provided its current status is NOT "Resolved" or "Rejected". Once resolved, details are locked for audit purposes.
